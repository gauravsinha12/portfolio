## The problem

I wanted a 7B model that argues a position well — takes a stance, defends it, and rebuts. Full fine-tuning a 7B model is out of reach on a single consumer GPU: the optimizer states and gradients alone dwarf the card's memory. QLoRA makes it tractable by freezing a 4-bit-quantized base model and training only small low-rank adapters, so the trainable footprint is a fraction of the full weights.

The base weights are quantized to 4-bit and frozen. The debate dataset is formatted into instruction/response pairs. Training updates only the LoRA adapters via PEFT and TRL. At inference, the same 4-bit base runs with the trained adapters applied.

## Engineering decisions

### QLoRA over full fine-tuning

Full fine-tuning of a 7B model holds full-precision weights, gradients, and optimizer state in memory at once — tens of gigabytes, more than a single consumer card has. QLoRA keeps the base weights frozen in 4-bit and trains only low-rank adapters, collapsing the trainable-parameter count and the optimizer footprint by orders of magnitude. The tradeoff is a modest quality ceiling versus a full fine-tune and some quantization noise.
